RICHMOND, Va. (WWBT) - A rally for gun rights took over The Virginia State Capitol Complex Saturday afternoon.

The Rally for Virginia drawing a few hundred who support second amendment freedom and organized by Micah Goodman.

“These rights were inherent to us and given to us by nature’s god and something that Abigail Spanberger has not realized she’s misplaced her authority because she is not a god,” said Micah Goodman, rally organizer.

The hour-long event included republican lawmakers as well as social media influencers, all supporting second amendment rights.

“It means a mother protecting her children has the ability to defend her family and that’s why so many women believe this is not just a constitutional issue. It’s a safety issue,” said Lydia Shaffer, Conservative Barbie.

Some of that legislation in question includes safe storage requirements, tightening limits on guns in state buildings and college campuses and a ban on the sale of assault weapons and high-capacity magazines. All passed by democrats in control of the Virginia General Assembly.

Democrats say the legislation makes communities safer.

Those in the crowd disagree.

“Crime is just and excuse for the actual end goal of gun control which is civilian disarmament. That’s the end goal. Complete and total civilian disarmament,” said Philip Van Cleave, VCDL President.

Virginia Citizens Defense League President Phillip Van Cleve says his group will challenge the legislation, if it becomes law, in the courts.

Heightened security around the grounds.

No guns allowed on the property.

But through the fence line along 9th Street people were allowed to open carry and they did and handed out magazines to those who wanted one.

“But because god gave you legitimate instincts that say you should defend yourself to the same degree in which people want to offend you,” said Ross the Boss, event speaker.

All the gun legislation that made it out of the statehouse is now on the desk of the governor.

A spokesperson for Abigail Spanberger says she hasn’t taken any action on those bills but is reviewing.
