# EACC arrests former judge in alleged Sh10 . 4m bribery probe

The Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) has

arrested a former judge over an alleged Sh10.4 million

bribery scheme linked to an ongoing commercial dispute before the High Court.

In a statement on Monday, the anti-graft agency said the

arrest was part of ongoing investigations into allegations that the suspects

demanded USD 80,000 (about Sh10.4 million) to influence the outcome of a case

involving a former Cabinet minister.

The commission said the former judge was arrested alongside

an advocate of the High Court and two other suspects as detectives moved to

disrupt the alleged bribery scheme.

According to the EACC, the money was allegedly solicited to

interfere with proceedings in a commercial dispute currently pending before the

High Court.

The suspects are being held at the EACC Integrity Centre

Police Station in Nairobi as investigators finalise processing and prepare the

next steps in the case.

EACC said the suspects are expected to undergo further

processing on Tuesday as investigations continue.

EACC is a public

body established under Section 3(1) of the Ethics and Anti-Corruption

Commission Act, 2011.

The commission is composed of a chairperson and four

commissioners appointed in accordance with the Constitution and Section 4 of

the EACC Act. It operates through a secretariat headed by the Secretary and chief executive officer.

EACC’s mandate is to combat and prevent corruption, economic

crimes and unethical conduct in Kenya through law enforcement, prevention

initiatives, public education, and the promotion of integrity and ethical

standards.

The commission gathers information on corruption in

government and the public sector from various sources, including members of the

public, government officials, institutions and the media.

Kenyans can report suspected corruption cases at the EACC

headquarters at the Integrity Centre in Nairobi, through its regional offices, or at

Huduma Centres across the country.
