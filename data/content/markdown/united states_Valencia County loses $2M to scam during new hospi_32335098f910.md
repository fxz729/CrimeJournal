Valencia County loses $2M to scam during new hospital construction

Valencia County is out millions after an employee fell for a scam involving a construction contractor.

VALENCIA COUNTY, N.M. – Valencia County is out millions after an employee fell for a scam involving a construction contractor.

A scammer reportedly posed as Bradbury Stamm Construction, the contractor for the new Valencia County Hospital in Los Lunas. The county paid over $2 million before discovering the fraud.

The county contacted their bank, the state auditor and legal authorities immediately after realizing the scam. A third-party investigation was conducted to determine how it happened and prevent future occurrences.

“Even with thorough technological training, robust testing as well as internal processes, a mistake happened as a result of human error,” said County Manager Jhonathan Aragon in a statement.

Aragon also mentioned that the incident does not impact the hospital construction, which is still expected to open this fall.
