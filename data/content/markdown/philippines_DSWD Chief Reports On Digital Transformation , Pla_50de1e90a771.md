Department of Social Welfare and Development

(

DSWD

) Secretary Rex Gatchalian has reported the DSWD’s accomplishments, highlighting digital transformation, expansion of innovative programs and streamlined public service as among the 2025 gains of the agency.

Among the programs cited by the DSWD chief was the Walang Gutom Program (WGP), the national government’s anti-hunger initiative wherein food-poor families are provided with electronic benefit transfer (EBT) cards with Php3,000 in food credits to purchase select nutritious food items from accredited retailers.

“We’ve already scaled up yung Walang Gutom program natin to 600,000 food poor families. Mukha kasing maliit ang numero 600,000 pero that’s 600,000 na pamilya na kumakalam ang sikmura nang matutulog,” Secretary Gatchalian said on DZMM’s ‘Serbisyong DSWD for Every Juan’ on Saturday (March 14).

In accordance with the directive of the President to digitalize government services to make it more accessible, the DSWD has also automated several frontline services, including the processing of permits for minors traveling abroad (MTA), making it more efficient while safeguarding children from human trafficking.

“Dati-rati mano-mano yan. Pupunta ka sa opisina ng DSWD, luluwas ka, kuha ka ng permit buong araw, wala ka na sa trabaho. Pero alinsunod sa utos ng ating pangulo, kailangan lahat as easy as 1-2-3. So ngayon pwede ka kumuha ng permit pag ang anak mo mag-travel mag-isa online na lang,” Secretary Gatchalian told DZMM anchors.

Digitalization is also transforming the country’s largest social protection program — the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps) — which accounts for nearly half of the agency’s annual budget. The DSWD is also transitioning grant payouts to digital platforms through a partnership with private sector institutions.

The conditional cash transfer program now utilizes mobile financial services, including the digital wallet platform GCash, with support from Globe Telecom, Ayala Foundation, BPI Foundation, and Landbank to provide financial literacy training and access to affordable mobile devices.

“Yung 4Ps lumilipat na tayo sa GCash at gusto kong ipaliwanag na walang binili yung Departamento na cellphone. Galing sa Globe Telecom, Ayala Foundation, and GCash lahat…so they do the digital financial literacy, paano hindi maloko online. They raise second hand phones and convert it to cheaper first hand phones, ” Secretary Gatchalian said.

Alongside digital wallets, the DSWD is developing a new software system that allows beneficiaries to upload school report cards and health records directly, significantly reducing the waiting time for compliance verification and grant release.

Beyond digital transformation, the DSWD is also expanding several social protection initiatives in 2026– these include Project SAFE, an advocacy campaign promoting online safety for children;

ProtecTEEN

, a holistic program supporting teenage mothers and preventing early pregnancy; and Project ARUGA, which provides support for families raising children with special needs.

The DSWD is also continuously implementing the Pag-abot Program, which reaches out to families and individuals in street situations (FISS).

The program has already profiled around 18,000 individuals living in the streets of Metro Manila, with more than 13,000 individuals agreeing to receive government intervention.

The DSWD has already expanded the program, establishing a Pag-abot processing center in Central Visayas. (KI)
