# CBI arrests proclaimed offender in 24 - year - old bank fraud case | Central India Premier English Daily

New Delhi, March 15 (UNI)

The Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) on Sunday said that it has arrested Harpal Singh Ahuja, a proclaimed offender, in connection with a 24-year-old bank fraud case.

A senior CBI official said that the agency registered the case in 2001, and, after completion of the investigation, a chargesheet was filed in 2003 against the accused.

Ahuja, the main conspirator in the case, absconded during the trial proceedings in 2014. He was declared a proclaimed offender by a special court in Ghaziabad in the same year.

An open-dated arrest warrant was also issued by the court in 2017.

The trial proceedings against the other accused persons have already been concluded, but the trial against accused Harpal Singh Ahuja, which requires his presence before the court, is still pending.

During the 11 years while evading justice, Ahuja frequently changed his locations between Mumbai and Faridabad. After sustained efforts, the accused was finally traced in Mumbai and arrested in the Kalyan area.

“The accused was brought and produced before the Special Judge, CBI Court, Ghaziabad, who remanded him to judicial custody,” the CBI official said.
