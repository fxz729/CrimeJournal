The book also says that Prince Harry feared his older brother and future king, William, ‘could remove all the Sussexes’ titles and effectively banish him from Britain’.

Responding to the allegations, a spokesperson for Prince Harry and Meghan said Bower had ‘long crossed the line from criticism into fixation’.

They added: ‘This is someone who has publicly stated, “the monarchy in fact depends on actually obliterating the Sussexes from our state of life,” language that speaks for itself.

‘He has made a career out of constructing ever more elaborate theories about people he does not know and has never met.

‘Those interested in facts will look elsewhere; those seeking deranged conspiracy and melodrama know exactly where to find him.’

This is not the royal couple’s first run-in with Bower, who has written controversial books on the UK’s most famous faces.

Who is Tom Bower?

Tom Bower moved from BBC journalist to investigative biographies (Picture: Ken McKay/ITV/Shutterstock)

Tom Bower, 79, is a former BBC

Panorama

journalist who is known for his unauthorised biographies.

He left the

BBC

in 1995 and has written more than 20 books chronicling the lives of Royals, politicians and British tycoons.

His first works focused on the failure to denazify post war

Germany

and the hunt for Nazi war criminals.

He then turned his attention to publishing giant Robert Maxwell and, later, business magnate Richard Branson.

Both men unsuccessfully sued the author, with Maxwell pursuing him in court on multiple occasions.

Bower has sold more than two million books, many of which have been critically acclaimed.

Sir Richard Branson was the focus of one of Bower’s books

(Credits: Ken McKay/ITV/REX/Shutterstock)

His work on corruption in English football, Broken Dreams: Vanity, Greed and the Souring of British Football, received the William Hill Sports Book of the Year prize.

Not all of his books have won him enemies. He has said he’s now friends with Simon Cowell and Bernie Ecclestone after writing biographies on both of them.

What else has Tom Bower written about the royal family?

The royals have been repeated targets for Bower’s investigative journalism.

One work published in 2018 – The Rebel Prince -tells the story of Prince Charles’s bid to rehabilitate himself after Princess Diana’s death.

The highly negative biography was a number one best seller – portraying the now King as vain, cold and out of touch.

In 2022, Bower has already published a book on Meghan Markle and Prince Harry.

King Charles was portrayed as vain and out of touch by Bower when he was the Prince of Wales (Picture: Max Mumby/Indigo/Getty Images)

Titled Revenge, he wrote about their strained relationship with the Royal Family.

The book claimed the Duchess of Sussex rejected a suggestion by the Queen and the Prince of Wales that she fly to the US to reconcile with her father.

In an interview with the Telegraph in

2023
