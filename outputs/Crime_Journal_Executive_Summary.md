# Crime Journal - Executive Summary

## Project Overview

Crime Journal is an AI-powered global crime database and intelligent retrieval system designed for lawyers, police officers, and legal researchers. The platform leverages GDELT's real-time global news data to provide comprehensive, multilingual crime case research capabilities.

## Key Findings (Validated with 666 Real Cases)

### 1. Data Scale & Coverage

- **Total Cases**: 666 cases (7-day collection period)
- **Language Coverage**: 46 languages
- **Country Coverage**: 57 countries
- **News Sources**: 200+ domains

### 2. Language Distribution

- **Spanish dominates**: 28.4% (189 cases)
- **English only 15.5%**: Validates non-English case demand
- **Chinese**: 8.1% (54 cases)
- **Non-English total**: 84.5%

### 3. Geographic Distribution

- **Top 5 Countries**: Spain (12.3%), China (8.1%), Argentina (6.3%), USA (5.7%), Vietnam (5.3%)
- **Europe**: 38% combined share
- **Asia**: 25% combined share

### 4. Crime Type Distribution

- Fraud: 12.2% (81 cases)
- Trafficking: 9.0% (60 cases)
- Assault: 8.1% (54 cases)
- Murder: 7.5% (50 cases)
- Drug crimes: 6.0% (40 cases)
- Requires deep AI analysis: 60.7%

### 5. Content Quality

- **Average length**: 6,449 characters (~3,000-4,000 words)
- **Median**: 3,364 characters
- **Max**: 424,546 characters
- **Min**: 236 characters

### 6. Real-Time Validation

- **Same-day cases**: 76.9% (512 cases)
- **3-day cases**: 86.6%
- **7-day cases**: 100%
- **Validates 15-minute update value**
